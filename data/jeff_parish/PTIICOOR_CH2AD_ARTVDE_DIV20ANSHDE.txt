The animal shelter department and the position of director of the animal shelter department are hereby created, which department and position shall have direct administrative supervision over the animal shelter and animal welfare and control functions of the parish, including but not limited to the enforcement of animal welfare ordinances and for the collection, impounding, seizing or holding, care and disposal of animals in the parish.
(Ord. No. 18471, § 4, 2-19-92; Ord. No. 20257, § 1, 1-14-98)

(a)
Qualifications. The director of the animal shelter department shall be qualified by education, training, and experience relative to the animal welfare/control/shelter profession and shall devote his/her entire efforts to the purpose for which this department has been created. The director of the animal shelter shall possess the following qualifications:
(1)
A bachelor's degree supplemented by at least three (3) years of paid professional administrative and/or management experience, which includes two (2) years relative to animal welfare/control/shelter profession or related experience; or
(2)
An equivalent combination of education, management and ten (10) years experience in the field of animal control or welfare.
(b)
Appointment. The director of the animal shelter department shall be appointed by the parish president with the approval of the council.
(c)
Salary. The salary of the director of animal shelter shall be established by the parish president within the pay grade for the position as defined in the executive pay plan.
(Ord. No. 18471, § 4, 2-19-92; Ord. No. 20257, § 1, 1-14-98; Ord. No. 25736 , § 28, 2-6-19; Ord. No. 25818 , § 1, 7-24-19)

Sec. 2-408. - Duties of the director.

The director of the animal shelter department shall:
(1)
Manage department operations of animal welfare and control and the animal shelter;
(2)
Provide policy guidance to the president in the area of animal welfare/control/public health;
(3)
Direct administrative and field staff persons assigned to this department;
(4)
Formulate, implement and evaluate operating policies, procedures and programs relative to animal welfare/control/public health services with the advice and consent of the president;
(5)
Prepare reports on departmental operations, evaluating performance against established objectives, and special reports on operating problems or plans as required for review by the president;
(6)
Coordinate budget formulation and management activities in the animal shelter department;
(7)
Coordinate activities with animal shelter advisory board and its mandated authorities.
(Ord. No. 18471, § 4, 2-19-92; Ord. No. 20257, § 1, 1-14-98)
