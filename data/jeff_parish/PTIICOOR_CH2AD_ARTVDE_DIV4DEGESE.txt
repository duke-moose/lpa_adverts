Sec. 2-167. - Qualifications, appointment and salary of director.

(a)
Qualifications. The general services director shall be qualified by education, training and at least five (5) years of related administrative and/or management experience and shall devote his entire efforts to the purpose for which this office has been created.
(b)
Appointment. The general services director shall be appointed by the parish president with the approval of the council.
(c)
Salary. The salary of the director of general services shall be established by the parish president within the pay grade for the position as defined in the executive pay plan.
(Code 1961, §§ 2-271, 2-273; Ord. No. 21171, § 8, 1-10-01; Ord. No. 25736 , § 8, 2-6-19)
Editor's note— Ord. No. 25736 , § 8, adopted Feb. 6, 2019, amended § 2-167 and in so doing changed the title from "Qualifications and appointment of director" to "Qualifications, appointment and salary of director," as set out herein.

The director of general services shall be responsible for:
(1)
The responsibility for administration of the maintenance and operation of public buildings under the budget of the department of general services;
(2)
The centralized administration of other intragovernmental services, including, but not limited to, messenger services and mail services;
(3)
The centralized control and disposal of all surplus movable properties shall be the full responsibility of the department of general services.
(Code 1961, § 2-272; Ord. No. 15431, § 1, 2-9-83; Ord. No. 21171, § 9, 1-10-01; Ord. No. 25975 , § 1, 4-8-20)
