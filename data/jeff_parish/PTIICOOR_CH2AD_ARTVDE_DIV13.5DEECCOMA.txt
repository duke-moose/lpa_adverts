There is hereby created the department of ecosystem and coastal management and the position of director of ecosystem and coastal management to serve the parish, oversee ecosystem and coastal restoration and management responsibilities in Jefferson Parish, and provide an integrated, holistic approach to the restoration and management of the coastal and floodplain ecosystems and environments that comprise Jefferson Parish.
( Ord. No. 25964 , § 1, 3-11-20)
Sec. 2-305. - Qualifications, appointment, and salary for director of ecosystem and coastal management.

(a)
Qualification. The Director of Ecosystem and Coastal Management shall be qualified by education with a minimum of a bachelor's degree from an accredited college or university in environmental engineering or science, business, or a related field, and five (5) years coastal zone or floodplain management experience to direct and coordinate the various purposes for which the office has been created.
(b)
Appointment. The director of ecosystem and coastal management shall be appointed by the parish president with the approval of the council.
(c)
Salary. The salary of the director of ecosystem and coastal management shall be established by the parish president within the pay grade for the position as defined in the executive pay plan.
( Ord. No. 25964 , § 1, 3-11-20)
Sec. 2-306. - Duties of the director of ecosystem and coastal management—Generally.

The director of ecosystem and coastal management's duties shall include but are not limited to the following:
(a)
Hire, train, and manage personnel; assign tasks and projects; monitor field work; evaluate employee work performance, including any salary increases, disciplinary action/discharges, and employee transfers and promotions; approve leave requests and enforce administrative management policies and personnel rules.
(b)
Develop coastal restoration and protection strategies for the Parish's diverse ecosystem.
(c)
Coordinate the parish's interaction with the public, federal and state agencies involved in comprehensive planning activities for coastal restoration and management to maximize the funding of projects benefitting Jefferson Parish.
(d)
Provide oversight and coordinates with the department of floodplain management to ensure integrated ecosystem management.
(e)
Researches, prepares applications and manages all grants and projects with local and federal agencies including, but not limited to, RESTORE Act and GOMESA funds allocated to the parish.
(f)
Provides leadership and direction in the development and subsequent implementation of short and long range plans; gathers, interprets and prepares data for studies, reports, and recommendations which include the Jefferson Parish Coastal Master Plan.
(g)
Coordinate department activities with other parish departments and outside agencies.
(h)
Provide professional recommendations to the parish president, parish council, other elected officials and state and federal agencies regarding coastal protection and restoration and associated environmental issues in the parish.
(i)
Communicate official plans, policies and regulatory procedures to the general public, business and industry and elected officials.
(j)
Participate in the meetings of the Coastal Protection and Restoration Authority (CPRA); Coastal Wetlands Planning Protection and Restoration Act (CWPPRA) Task Force and Technical Committee, and relevant meetings of other ecosystem planning and management entities in the region and state.
(k)
Perform public information/promotional services through the development of coastal protection, restoration, and enhancement public service announcements, brochures, television and radio appearances, and other publicity efforts; and address various civic and business organizations.
(l)
Promote the education of Jefferson Parish residents on ecosystem and coastal restoration and management issues.
( Ord. No. 25964 , § 1, 3-11-20)
