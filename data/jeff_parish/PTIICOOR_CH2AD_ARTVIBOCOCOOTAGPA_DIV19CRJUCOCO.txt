(a) The parish council hereby appoints the following members to the criminal justice coordinating council:
Parish president; council member at-large, Division A, parish council; council member at-large, Division B, parish council; sheriff; clerk of court; district attorney; Twenty-Fourth Judicial District Court, chief judge; chief judge, parish juvenile court; one (1) judge of parish courts to be selected by first and second parish courts; one (1) justice of the peace to be selected on an annual basis by all justices of the peace in the parish; chairman of the indigent defender board; Chief of Police, City of Gretna; Chief of Police, City of Kenner; Chief of Police, City of Harahan, and Chief of Police of the City of Westwego; one (1) constable to be selected on an annual basis by all constables in the parish; the executive director of the Jefferson Parish Human Services Authority and the superintendent of the Jefferson Parish School System; and the Jefferson Parish Coroner, or their designees. The terms of the public officials shall expire upon their termination of office.
(b)
The parish council shall appoint other members so that its membership shall be broadly representative of the general public, and community-based and professional organizations.
(c)
The criminal justice coordinating council shall consist of twenty-five (25) members and of the twenty-five (25) members, one (1) member shall be appointed from the unincorporated area of each council district by the parish council.
(Code 1961, § 2-862(a), (b), (d); Ord. No. 15704, §§ 1, 2, 8-24-83; Ord. No. 18462, § 5, 2-5-92; Ord. No. 19352, § 1, 3-22-95; Ord. No. 19899, § 1, 12-11-96; Ord. No. 20785, § 1, 9-22-99; Ord. No. 20901, § 1, 2-23-00; Ord. No. 20932, § 1, 4-12-00; Ord. No. 22145, § 1, 3-17-04)

The criminal justice coordinating council shall have the following functions and duties:
(1)
The criminal justice coordinating council shall have no independent staff but shall have the authority to contract with the Parish of Jefferson to provide staff assistance necessary to carry out the purposes for which the criminal justice coordinating council was created;
(2)
To assist and advise units of local government and officers in the application and receipt of grants for the implementation of criminal justice programs. Such application of grant requests shall be consistent with the priorities expressed in the Parish Criminal Justice Master Plan;
(3)
Be designated by the Jefferson Parish Council to accept grants on behalf of the parish criminal justice system and to distribute funds received to the appropriate recipients.
(Code 1961, § 2-863; Ord. No. 16083, § 1, 6-20-84; Ord. No. 20307, § 2, 3-25-98)
