The department of electronic information systems management and the position of director of electronic information systems management is hereby created. Which department and position shall have direct administrative supervision over all functions, duties, and responsibilities of data processing, geographic information systems, and coordination of all other parish computer operations including internal operations, equipment hardware and software, functional department and service operations and future modifications and/or expansion of existing and new systems.
(Ord. No. 19649, § 2, 2-28-96)

(a)
[Qualifications generally.] The director of the department of electronic information systems management shall possess the following qualifications:
(1)
A thorough knowledge of governmental information systems and services, demonstrates an ability to manage complex operations, possess skills in computer analysis, have proven oral and written communication skills and demonstrate leadership skills in a local government setting. Director of electronic information systems management shall also possess the following qualifications:
(2)
A Bachelors Degree from an accredited college or university with significant paid satisfactory work experience in the management of electronic information systems within a government setting, and otherwise be qualified by training and other administrative and technical management experience and shall devote his entire efforts to this purpose for which this department has been created.
(b)
Appointment. The director of electronic information systems management shall be appointed by the parish president with the approval of the council.
(c)
Salary. The salary of the director of electronic information systems management shall be established by the parish president within the pay grade for the position as defined in the executive pay plan.
(Ord. No. 19649, § 2, 2-28-96; Ord. No. 25736 , § 14, 2-6-19)
Editor's note— Ord. No. 25736 , § 14, adopted Feb. 6, 2019, amended § 2-230 and in so doing changed the title from "Qualifications" to "Qualifications, appointment and salary of director," as set out herein.
Sec. 2-231. - Duties of the director.

The director of electronic information systems management shall:
(1)
Manage department operations of the electronic information systems management department and the office of telecommunications;
(2)
Direct staff persons assigned to the department, including hiring, termination, enforcement of work rules and regulations, delegation of department work assignments and other related administrative/management duties and responsibilities;
(3)
Provide policy guidance to the president and deputy C.A.A. in the area of electronic information systems;
(4)
Plan, formulate, develop, and evaluate operating policies, procedures and programs relating to use of various parish electronic information systems;
(5)
Member of the parish data processing advisory committee;
(6)
Negotiate and oversee any and all business activities with contracted computer related vendors and computer software providers;
(7)
Provide leadership for G.I.S./SCADA operations, data processing operations, and other related parish electronic information systems;
(8)
Interface with public works as it relates to G.I.S./SCADA operations and services;
(9)
Coordinate budget formulation and management activities in the electronic information systems management department;
(10)
Prepare reports on departmental operations evaluating performance against established objectives, and special reports relative to electronic information systems;
(11)
Serve as primary liaison to the government and non-government users of parish's electronic information systems;
(12)
May represent parish president or deputy C.A.A. at meetings relative to electronic information systems;
(13)
Conduct field inspections, visit and observe electronic information systems operations; and
(14)
Direct the operations of the telecommunications systems.
(Ord. No. 19649, § 2, 2-28-96; Ord. No. 25398 , § 2, 7-12-17)
