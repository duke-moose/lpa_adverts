(a)
Qualifications. The director of drainage shall be qualified by a four-year degree from an accredited college or university with degree in engineering and current registration as a professional engineer in the State of Louisiana plus five (5) years of engineering experience in the design and construction management of drainage projects, or ten (10) years of equivalent engineering experience with public works agency or consulting firm of which five (5) years' experience in the design and construction management of drainage projects.
(b)
Appointment. The director of drainage shall be appointed by the parish president with the approval of the council.
(c)
Salary. The salary of the director of drainage shall be established by the parish president within the pay grade for the position as defined in the executive pay plan.
(Code 1961, § 2-432; Ord. No. 22460, § 1, 3-23-05; Ord. No. 25736 , § 17, 2-6-19)
Editor's note— Ord. No. 25736 , § 17, adopted Feb. 6, 2019, amended § 2-257 and in so doing changed the title from "Qualifications and appointment of director" to "Qualifications, appointment and salary of director," as set out herein.
Sec. 2-258. - Duties of the director generally.

The director of drainage shall:
(1)
Assist the public works director in the responsibility of drainage and control of storm waters to include canals, pumping stations, and all related structures within the boundaries of the parish;
(2)
Direct engineering personnel and field superintendents in planning and implementing drainage and flood control system project schedules;
(3)
Assist the department of capital projects in establishing scope of work and design criteria for drainage capital projects;
(4)
Direct engineers and consultants regarding department projects scope and design criteria;
(5)
Inspect operation/maintenance and construction projects;
(6)
Provide department policy and management guidance in all areas of drainage and pumping station operations;
(7)
Evaluate department operations for all areas of drainage and pumping stations and report to the parish president and the director of public works on drainage department performance;
(8)
Coordinate budget formulation activities for the department of drainage; coordinate annual five-year capital projects budget; review and authorize expenditures;
(9)
Direct the department of drainage in formulating and evaluating operating policies, programs and procedures and effective and efficient job performance;
(10)
Prepare reports on departmental operations evaluating performance against established objectives, and special reports on operating problems or plans as required;
(11)
Prepare policy statements and plans for the department of drainage under the direction of the director of public works;
(12)
Be responsible for the administrative management of operations and maintenance of all drainage department facilities and pumping stations;
(13)
Serve as department appointing authority and fulfill all duties and responsibilities required of an appointing authority.
(Code 1961, § 2-431; Ord. No. 17555, § 2, 7-13-88; Ord. No. 22460, § 1, 3-23-05)
