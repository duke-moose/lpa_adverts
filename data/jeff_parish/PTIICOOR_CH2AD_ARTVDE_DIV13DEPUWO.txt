The department of public works and the position of director of public works are hereby created which department and position shall have direct administrative supervision over the sewerage, water, drainage, streets, engineering and parkway functions of the parish.
(Code 1961, § 2-450; Ord. No. 17552, § 2, 7-13-88)

(a)
Qualifications. The director of public works shall possess the following qualifications:
(1)
A four-year degree in engineering with eight (8) years' relevant experience and active registration as a professional engineer in the state;
(2)
Five (5) years of experience as an assistant city manager or department director with comparable experience; and
(3)
Ten (10) years of significant management experience with a public works agency.
(b)
Appointment. The director of public works shall be appointed by the parish president with the approval of the council.
(c)
Salary. The salary of the director of public works shall be established by the parish president within the pay grade for the position as defined in the executive pay plan.
(Code 1961, § 2-452; Ord. No. 17552, § 3, 7-13-88; Ord. No. 24723, § 1, 4-9-14; Ord. No. 25736 , § 21, 2-6-19)
Editor's note— Ord. No. 25736 , § 21, adopted Feb. 6, 2019, amended § 2-302 and in so doing changed the title from "Qualifications and appointment of director" to "Qualifications, appointment and salary of director," as set out herein.
Sec. 2-303. - Duties of director.

The director of public works shall:
(1)
Be responsible for all public works functions including administration, financial operation, construction, maintenance and policy development in the areas of water, sewerage, drainage, streets, engineering, including traffic engineering, parkways, capital projects, flood plain management; and environmental affairs;
(2)
Evaluate department operations for public works and environmental affairs and report to the parish president and parish council on department performance on a regular basis;
(3)
Be responsible for capital facilities planning;
(4)
Counsel with the parish president and the parish council on policy decisions affecting public works and environmental affairs;
(5)
Direct administrative and laboratory support staff;
(6)
Direct the public works group and department of environmental affairs in formulating and evaluating operating policies, programs and procedures;
(7)
Prepare reports on departmental operations evaluating performance against established objectives, and special reports on operating problems or plans as required;
(8)
Coordinate budget formulation activities in the public works group and department of environmental affairs;
(9)
Prepare policy statements and plans for the public works group and department of environmental affairs under the direction of the parish president.
(Code 1961, § 2-451; Ord. No. 17552, § 4, 7-13-88; Ord. No. 24723, § 2, 4-9-14)
