A department to be known as juvenile services and the position of director of juvenile services are hereby created which department and position shall be responsible for operating both residential and community services for juvenile offenders.
(Ord. No. 21662, § 2, 9-11-02)
Sec. 2-510.1 - Qualifications, appointment and salary of director.

(a)
Qualifications. The director of juvenile services shall be qualified by education and training, possess a thorough knowledge of the juvenile justice system and relevant research in this field, demonstrate an ability to manage administrative operations and supervise staffs, have demonstrated skills in program development, have proven oral and written communication skills, demonstrate leadership ability, be familiar with operations of residential facilities, and be committed to treatment-oriented community-based programming for juvenile offenders. The director of juvenile services shall also have the requisite educational and work experiences.
(b)
Appointment. The director of juvenile services shall be appointed by the parish president with the approval of the parish council.
(c)
Salary. The salary of the director of juvenile services shall be established by the parish president within the pay grade for the position as defined in the executive pay plan.
(Ord. No. 21662, § 2, 9-11-02; Ord. No. 25736 , § 39, 2-6-19)
Editor's note— Ord. No. 25736 , § 39, adopted Feb. 6, 2019, amended § 2-510.1 and in so doing changed the title from "Qualifications and appointment of director" to "Qualifications, appointment and salary of director," as set out herein.
Sec. 2-510.2. - Duties of the director of juvenile services.

The duties of the director shall include but not be limited to the following:
(1)
Operate the Rivarde Juvenile Detention Facility and any other residential facilities that may be developed;
(2)
Develop and operate a multi-disciplinary juvenile assessment center on the Rivarde site, in cooperation with other government officers and agencies;
(3)
Develop a community services unit, which will incorporate and merge all existing and/or perspective components of community supervision to ensure there exists an appropriate continuum of services and sanctions for juvenile offenders and families in need of services.
(4)
Coordinate with the director of the community justice agency and the director of administrative and planning services on issues of master planning, research, evaluation, and funding.
(Ord. No. 21662, § 2, 9-11-02)
