Sec. 7-218. - Definitions.

For the purposes of this chapter, the following terms and phrases when used in this article shall have the respective meanings ascribed to them in this section, unless a definite meaning clearly appears from the context:
Food shall include but not be limited to bread products, corn, fruit, oats, hay, nuts, wheat, alfalfa, salt blocks, feed, grain, vegetables, & commercially-sold wildlife feed or livestock feed and shall exclude live, naturally-growing shrubs, live crops, plants, flowers, vegetation, gardens, ornamental landscaping, trees, & fruit/nuts that have naturally-fallen on the ground or are within reach for consumption by waterfowl.
Waterfowl shall mean unowned and/or undomesticated ducks, geese, & other amphibious birds, specifically including but not limited to Muscovy ducks (Cairina moschata).
( Ord. No. 25896 , § 1, 11-6-19)
Sec. 7-219. - Feeding prohibited.

(a)
Except as provided in section 7-220 below, it shall be prohibited to harm, harass, and/or intentionally feed waterfowl by making food available for consumption on private property or on public property within a radius of twenty (20) feet of a public street and/or residence within the territorial limits of Jefferson Parish.
(b)
A person shall be deemed to have purposely fed or caused waterfowl to be fed if the person places/tosses food in any form on the ground or within reach for consumption by waterfowl.
(c)
Violation of any provision of this section shall be deemed an offense.
( Ord. No. 25896 , § 1, 11-6-19)

The provisions of section 7-219 above shall not apply to:
(1)
The placement of food into feeders kept at a minimum of three (3) feet above the ground and designed/intended solely for use by squirrels, chipmunks, birds, or other fowl;
(2)
Any animal control officer, parish community cat program participant, veterinarian, peace officer, parish employee, or federal/state wildlife official who is acting pursuant to a lawfully-authorized program to treat, manage, capture, trap, hunt, or remove wildlife and/or who is acting within the scope of his/her granted authority;
(3)
Any individual possessing a valid Louisiana hunting license who is engaged in hunting and/or is setting out feed for the purpose of baiting/attracting game animals and who is otherwise in full compliance with the Louisiana Wildlife and Fisheries rules and regulations regarding such activities;
(4)
Any individual acting in the good-faith placement of feed for domestic livestock or food for domestic pets located on his/her private property. (There shall be a presumption of good faith in instances of appropriate animal feed/food placed on the ground where such animal is in residence and/or engaged in the Jefferson Parish community cat program.); and
(5)
Jefferson Parish parks & recreational areas (except as otherwise expressly prohibited), including but not limited to: designated bird sanctuaries; federal, state, and parish wildlife preserves, management areas, or levees bordering the Mississippi River or Lake Pontchartrain; and upon navigable waters where waterfowl may be present.
( Ord. No. 25896 , § 1, 11-6-19)

(a)
First-time offenders violating any provisions of this article shall receive a warning along with written information regarding the negative impacts of feeding waterfowl; second-time offenders shall be issued citations and subject to the prosecution thereof in the Jefferson Parish Bureau of Administrative Adjudication and a maximum fine of one hundred dollars ($100.00); third offenses and any offenses thereafter shall be subject to a maximum fine of five hundred dollars ($500.00) per offense.
(b)
Nothing in this section shall be construed to limit any civil action that parish or individuals may take to enforce the provisions of this article.
( Ord. No. 25896 , § 1, 11-6-19)
