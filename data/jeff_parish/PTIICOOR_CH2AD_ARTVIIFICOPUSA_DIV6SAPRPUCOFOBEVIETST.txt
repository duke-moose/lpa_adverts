Sec. 2-986. - Definitions.

For purposes of this division, the following words shall have the following meanings:
(1)
Governmental and ethics compliance and audit committee shall mean the committee of that name as established under Chapter 2, Article VI, Division 2 of the Jefferson Parish Code of Ordinances.
(2)
Jefferson Parish contractor or parish contractor shall mean any person or business which is party to a contract with the Parish of Jefferson or with any of parish's political subdivisions, including, but not limited to, any parish agencies, divisions or special districts, whether or not said contract was awarded on a bid or a nonbid basis. "Jefferson Parish contractor" or "parish contractor" shall also include any subcontractor, principal, owner, employee, lobbyist or agent of any such person or business.
(3)
Jefferson Parish Governmental Ethical Standards shall mean the provisions of Article V of Chapter 23 of the Jefferson Parish Code of Ordinances, or any successor legislation.
(4)
Louisiana Code of Governmental Ethics shall mean the provisions of Louisiana Revised Statutes Title 42, Sections 1101, et seq., or any successor legislation.
(5)
Public employee shall have the same meaning as ascribed to that term under the Louisiana Code of Governmental Ethics.
(6)
Public servant shall have the same meaning as ascribed to that term under the Louisiana Code of Governmental Ethics.
(Ord. No. 23786, § 1, 3-24-10; Ord. No. 24649, § 1, 1-15-14)
Sec. 2-987. - Violation of ethical standards by public contractors prohibited.

(a)
No Jefferson Parish contractor shall violate any of the provisions of the Louisiana Code of Governmental Ethics or of the Jefferson Parish Governmental Ethical Standards.
(b)
No Jefferson Parish contractor shall knowingly participate in any transaction which shall cause a Jefferson Parish public servant or a public employee of Jefferson Parish or a public employee of any agency, division or special district of Jefferson Parish to be in violation of any of the provisions of the Louisiana Code of Governmental Ethics or of the Jefferson Parish Governmental Ethical Standards.
(Ord. No. 23786, § 1, 3-24-10)
Sec. 2-988. - Procedures for sanctions for violation of ethical standards by public contractors in lieu of conviction for said violation.

(a)
Whenever a public servant of Jefferson Parish has knowledge of a possible violation of the provisions of section 2-987 by a parish contractor, he shall present any evidence of such violation of which he is in possession to the Jefferson Parish Attorney. The parish attorney shall investigate the alleged violation.
(b)
Should the parish attorney conclude after investigation that the parish contractor is more likely than not in violation of any of the provisions of section 2-987, he shall proceed as follows:
(1)
The parish attorney shall notify the parish contractor in writing by certified mail of the alleged violation(s) by said contractor of the provisions of section 2-987, which notice shall include the details of the alleged violation(s) and a summation of the evidence for said violation(s).
(2)
The notice of violation shall state that the parish contractor alleged to be in violation shall have an opportunity to present evidence in defense or in mitigation of the alleged violation(s) to the governmental and ethics compliance and audit committee.
(3)
The parish attorney shall set a date, time and location for a hearing before the governmental and ethics compliance and audit committee to hear and consider any evidence of the alleged violation(s) as presented by the parish attorney and to also hear and consider any evidence as presented by the parish contractor in defense or in mitigation of the alleged violation(s). The parish attorney shall give reasonable notice of the hearing to the parish contractor.
(4)
After consideration of all evidence presented, the governmental and ethics compliance and audit committee shall make a recommendation to the Jefferson Parish Council as to whether or not any contract between the parish contractor and Jefferson Parish should be terminated due to the parish contractor's alleged violation(s). Should the committee recommend that any such contract be terminated, the committee may also recommend that the parish contractor be prohibited from contracting with Jefferson Parish or with any of the parish's agencies, divisions or special districts for a specified term not less than two (2) years.
(5)
A resolution to adopt the recommendations of the governmental and ethics compliance and audit committee shall be presented to the Jefferson Parish Council at the next regularly scheduled meeting following the hearing.
(6)
The council may adopt the resolution with or without amendments or may cancel the resolution for reasons assigned by the council at the meeting at which the resolution is presented for consideration.
(Ord. No. 23786, § 1, 3-24-10)
Sec. 2-989. - Sanctions for violation of ethical standards by public contractors and procedures for said sanctions.

(a)
A finding that a parish contractor has violated any of the provisions of the Louisiana Code of Governmental Ethics or of the Jefferson Parish Governmental Ethical Standards shall be grounds for the immediate termination of any contract between Jefferson Parish and the parish contractor or subcontractor, or between Jefferson Parish and any person, firm or business for which the parish contractor is a principal, owner, employee, lobbyist or agent. In addition, the parish contractor shall be disqualified from contracting with the parish for a minimum of two (2) years after a finding of such violation and shall be assessed a penalty payable to the parish in the amount of ten thousand dollars ($10,000.00) assessed as liquidated damages for each such finding of a violation.
(b)
Any parish contractor whose contract with Jefferson Parish or with any of its agencies, divisions or special districts has been terminated following the procedures under section 2-988 shall be disqualified from contracting with the parish for a minimum of two (2) years after such termination and shall be assessed a penalty payable to the parish in the amount of ten thousand dollars ($10,000.00) assessed as liquidated damages for each instance for which the contractor is found to be in violation of the provisions of 2-987 by the governmental and ethics compliance and audit committee.
(c)
The conviction or plea of guilty in any state or federal court of any parish contractor on any charge of public corruption or breach of any laws pertaining to governmental ethics shall be grounds for the immediate termination of any contract between Jefferson Parish and the parish contractor or subcontractor, or between Jefferson Parish and any person, firm or business for which the parish contractor is a principal, owner, employee, lobbyist or agent. In addition, the parish contractor shall be disqualified from contracting with the parish for a minimum of five (5) years after such conviction or guilty plea.
(Ord. No. 23786, § 1, 3-24-10; Ord. No. 24649, § 2, 1-15-14)
