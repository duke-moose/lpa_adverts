A department to be known as adult supervision and corrections and the position of director of adult supervision and corrections are hereby created, which department and position shall have responsibility for operating all community-based programs for pre-trial and convicted adult offender populations (except the adult jail). The director shall be known as the "adult continuum manager."
(Ord. No. 21662, § 3, 9-11-02)

(a)
Qualifications. The director of adult supervision and corrections shall be qualified by education and training, possess a thorough knowledge of the adult criminal justice system and pre-trial services programs generally, demonstrate an ability to manage administrative operations and oversee program development, have proven oral and written communication skills, demonstrate leadership ability in a local government setting, be familiar with residential operations for adult offenders, and demonstrate a commitment to community-based programming for adult offenders. The director of adult supervision and corrections shall also have the requisite educational and work experiences.
(b)
Appointment. The director of adult supervision and corrections shall be appointed by the parish president with the approval of the parish council.
(c)
Salary. The salary of the director of adult supervision and corrections shall be established by the parish president within group II of the executive pay plan.
(Ord. No. 21662, § 3, 9-11-02)

The duties of the director shall include but not be limited to the following:
(1)
Develop and operate community-based programs for pre-trial and convicted offender populations;
(2)
Develop an offender assessment unit;
(3)
Develop a supervision services unit that includes, but is not limited to, programs to supervise and track offenders released pre-trial, home incarceration, and pre-trial diversion and counseling;
(4)
Develop and operate an intermediate sanctions facility;
(5)
Ensure that there exists an appropriate continuum of community-based services and sanctions for adult offenders; and
(6)
Coordinate with the director of the community justice agency and the director of administrative and planning services on issues of master planning, research, evaluation, and funding.
(Ord. No. 21662, § 3, 9-11-02)
