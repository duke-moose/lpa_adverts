(a)
Qualifications. The director of telecommunications must have five (5) years of highly responsible, professional level experience in the field of telecommunications. All experience must have been in the administrative or senior managerial capacity, with responsibility for the development, management and operation of a major division of a telecommunications organization network; to include Voice over Internet Protocol telephony, Session Initiation Protocol (SIP) trunking, Wide Area Networks, and video conferencing; must have a working knowledge of Enhanced 911 Service with ALI, ANI selective routing; must have a thorough knowledge of the Association of Public-Safety Communications Officers Project 25 (Phase I & Phase II) trunking radio systems; must have a thorough knowledge of public service commissioners rates and tariffs as they apply to parish telephone systems; must have a thorough knowledge of the Federal Communications Commission Rules and Regulations as they apply to local government agencies; must have thorough knowledge of consolidated fire, police, and emergency medical computer-aided dispatching; must have working knowledge of all radios and telephone equipment used in the parish.
(b)
Appointment. The director of telecommunications shall be appointed by the parish president with the approval of the council and shall devote his entire efforts to the purpose for which this department has been created and any other such activities and/or responsibilities as the parish president may direct.
(c)
Salary. The salary of the director of telecommunications shall be established by the parish president within the pay grade for the position as defined in the executive pay plan.
(Ord. No. 25398 , § 4, 7-12-17; Ord. No. 25736 , § 15, 2-6-19)
Editor's note— Ord. No. 25736 , § 15, adopted Feb. 6, 2019, amended § 2-233 and in so doing changed the title from "Qualifications and appointment of director" to "Qualifications, appointment and salary of director," as set out herein.
Sec. 2-234. - Duties of director.

The director of telecommunications will be responsible for:
(1)
Management and supervision over all coordination, planning, and installation of new Jefferson Parish radio/telephone systems, including telephone and radio maintenance services, data transmission networks, teletype and facsimile networks, satellite and microwave services, mobile telephone services, intercoms and electromechanical paging and public address systems, radio paging eight hundred (800) MHz trunked and eight hundred (800) MHz conventional systems, related communications systems;
(2)
Serves as the liaison between Jefferson Parish and the Sheriff of Jefferson Parish regarding the operation of the Jefferson Parish Enhanced 911 Computer-Aided Dispatch Center;
(3)
Assumes full responsibilities for all telecommunications systems and maintenance services operated by the parish;
(4)
Directs, oversees, and coordinates the activities of all areas that comprise the office of telecommunication services;
(5)
Directs the development of the parish network for communications, all centralized and individual agency telephone systems for the parish;
(6)
Directs the development of performance specifications for procurement through the parish ownership or leasing of all communications equipment and systems;
(7)
Assures that all long- and short-term planning is performed in accordance with overall telecommunications policy of the parish;
(8)
Supervises all telecommunication contracts to ensure compliance with specifications of contracts;
(9)
Meets with other parish officials, including representatives of major users, groups, and boards, to ensure that planning is done in accordance with their needs and requirements;
(10)
Maintains a thorough knowledge and overall awareness of developments and trends in all areas of telecommunications through study and peer group activities; and
(11)
Develops policies and procedures to be adhered to by all parish departments.
(Ord. No. 25398 , § 4, 7-12-17)

In addition to the duties and responsibilities listed in section 2-234 herein, the office of telecommunications shall compile and maintain a list of all gate access codes for all gated communities. The office of telecommunications shall update this list quarterly, each such update to be completed by no later than the 15th day of the months of January, April, July and October. The updates shall also include gate access codes for any new gated communities added by the office of telecommunications to its list of gated communities. The director shall make the updated lists of gate access codes available to the 9-1-1 Communications Center for distribution of the information to law enforcement, fire protection, hazardous materials or emergency medical personnel as needed to respond to emergencies.
(Ord. No. 25398 , § 4, 7-12-17)
