The department of inspection and code enforcement and the position of director of inspection and code enforcement is hereby created which department and position shall have direct administrative supervision on inspection and code enforcement functions of the parish.
(Code 1961, § 2-380; Ord. No. 18471, § 8, 2-19-92)

(a)
Qualifications. The director of inspection and code enforcement shall be qualified by:
(1)
A bachelor's degree in architecture; civil engineering; planning; a juris doctorate: a master's degree in public administration or a related field; supplemented by five (5) years of management and supervisory experience; or
(2)
An equivalent combination of ten (10) years of supervisory and management experience, education and training in related field, and; shall devote his entire efforts to this purpose for which this department has been created and any other such activities and/or responsibilities as the parish president may direct.
(b)
Appointment. The director of inspection and code enforcement shall be appointed by the parish president with the approval of the council.
(c)
Salary. The salary of the director of inspection and code enforcement shall be established by the parish president within the pay grade for the position as defined in the executive pay plan.
(Code 1961, § 2-382; Ord. No. 24939 , § 1, 4-15-15; Ord. No. 25736 , § 10, 2-6-19)
Editor's note— Ord. No. 25736 , § 10, adopted Feb. 6, 2019, amended § 2-197 and in so doing changed the title from "Qualifications and appointment of director" to "Qualifications, appointment and salary of director," as set out herein.
Sec. 2-198. - Duties of the director of the department of inspection and code enforcement—Generally.

The director of the department of inspection and code enforcement shall direct, control and supervise the functions of:
(1)
Plan review/permitting section of the department;
(2)
Regulatory section, including the building, electrical, mechanical, plumbing, and gas sections;
(3)
Dangerous building abatement section of the department;
(4)
Cashier's section;
(5)
Reports to the council, through the parish administration, on an as-requested basis, with a comprehensive report on the number of dangerous buildings in the parish by council districts, and remediation planned within the following three (3) months;
(6)
Ensure the enforcement of codes as mandated by the State of Louisiana, as well as local ordinances, including, but not limited to the International Residential Code, International Building Code, International Existing Building Code, International Mechanical Code, International Fuel Gas Code, Louisiana State Plumbing Code, and the National Electrical Code;
(7)
Ensure the enforcement of the Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA) regulations for private development within the parish;
(8)
Facilitate the activities of the board of standards and appeals, including case origination, scheduling and administration of meetings, payment to members, and providing clerical and technical support;
(9)
Facilitate the activities of the board of zoning adjustments, including case origination and technical support;
(10)
Serve as a standing member of the Jefferson Parish alcohol beverage permit review committee.
(Code 1961, § 2-381; Ord. No. 18907, § 5, 11-17-93; Ord. No. 21095, § 1, 10-4-00; Ord. No. 21095, § 2, 10-4-00; Ord. No. 21567, § 1, 5-8-02; Ord. No. 22478, § 8, 4-13-05; Ord. No. 23213, § 1, 12-12-07; Ord. No. 24104, § 1, 8-31-11; Ord. No. 24176, § 1, 12-7-11; Ord. No. 24939 , § 4-15-15)
