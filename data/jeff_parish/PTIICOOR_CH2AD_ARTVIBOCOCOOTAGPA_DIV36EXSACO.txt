The executive safety committee shall be composed of six (6) voting members as follows:
The deputy chief operating officer, public works director, chief administrative assistant of operations, chief administrative assistant of information, director of department of risk management, and the director of department of human resources management.
The safety program manager and security director shall serve as non-voting technical consultants to the committee and shall attend all meetings.

[The executive safety committee shall have the following duties and responsibilities:]
(1)
Identify employee and citizen safety and health issues within Jefferson Parish properties;
(2)
Develop strategic goals and objectives for parish wide safety and occupational health program;
(3)
Coordinate, direct, and assist implementation and enforcement of parish wide safety program;
(4)
Act as a consultant to senior management by continually identifying safety and health problems and recommending solutions.
(5)
Review findings of departmental accident review committees (which is to include information on accidents, injuries, loss); and review general liability, auto liability and workers compensation information by department.
(Ord. No. 20426, § 1E, 8-5-98; Ord. No. 25768 , § 3, 4-3-19)

To accomplish the responsibilities and duties, the executive safety committee establishes the following standing subcommittees:
(1)
Accident investigation. The committee's mission is to conduct investigation and assessment on Worker's Compensation, automobile and general liability accidents and make recommendations for corrective action to prevent reoccurrence;
(2)
Inspection and audit. The committee's mission is to conduct inspection and audit of parish facilities to locate accident causes and unsafe working practices and conditions and to determine corrective action as necessary;
(3)
Departmental safety committees. The committees' mission is to reduce injury and improve safety practices within the departments, review departmental accidents and make recommendations to the appointing authority on elimination of unsafe working conditions and practices, insure all elements of the safety program are conducted, that is, "tailgate" meetings, in house inspections and job specific hazard training in accordance with the safety manual;
(4)
Additional special subcommittees as needed. Each subcommittee shall provide a monthly report on accidents, injuries, loss and/or status to the executive safety committee.
Each subcommittee shall meet at least monthly as necessary to accomplish its assigned functions.
Each subcommittee shall provide a monthly report on accidents, injuries, loss and/or status to the executive safety committee.
(Ord. No. 20426, § 1F, 8-5-98)
