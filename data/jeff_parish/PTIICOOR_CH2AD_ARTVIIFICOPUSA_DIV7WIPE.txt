Sec. 2-996. - Eligibility.

(a)
In order for a widow to be eligible to obtain a widow's pension from the parish, her net income per month must not exceed three hundred fifty dollars ($350.00) if the widow is living in the home of a relative, four hundred dollars ($400.00) if the widow maintains her own residence, or if her social security allowance is her only source of income, and does not exceed four hundred twenty-five dollars ($425.00); no widows shall be added to the roster of the widow's pension after December 31, 1989.
(b)
All applicants must have at least three (3) years continuous residency in the parish prior to making application.
(Code 1961, §§ 13-11.8, 13-11.14; Ord. No. 17881, § 5, 11-29-89)
Editor's note— Ord. No. 17881, § 5, adopted November 29, 1989, which is set out herein as § 2-996 and prohibits any additional names from being added to the roster of qualified widows after December 31, 1989, also contained a clause which provided that the benefits of those persons who were on the roster as of December 31, 1989, would not be affected by the adoption of Ord. No. 17881.
Sec. 2-997. - Application; investigation.

(a)
It is hereby provided that all widows' pension applications will be investigated by an agency to be named by the parish council for the purpose of authenticating the information provided on all widows' pension application forms and the accompanying affidavits.
(b)
All future widows' pension applicants will be informed and made totally aware of the fact that all information provided on their application and accompanying affidavit will be investigated in order to provide a method of authenticating all the information provided by the applicant.
(c)
Any information provided by a widows' pension applicant on the widows' pension form or accompanying affidavit and found to be falsified by the appointed investigating agency shall provide grounds for rejection of the application by the parish council.
(Code 1961, §§ 13-11.11—13-11.13)
