It shall be the duty of all owners of property abutting streets to maintain the sidewalk space adjacent to or upon their property as provided by section 29-143, et seq. of the Code of Ordinances. It shall also be the duty of all property owners to maintain their driveways in a proper state of repair and free from hazardous conditions. Walks, driveways and similar paved areas of any premises shall be maintained in a reasonably clean and sanitary condition, free of any holes, substantial cracking or other hazard. Such areas shall also be swept clean of dirt and debris on a regular basis and otherwise maintained in a clean and sanitary condition.
(Ord. No. 23636, § 1, 9-2-09)

It shall be unlawful for any person or proprietary party to maintain, permit or allow on any premises, within one hundred fifty (150) feet of an existing building, the growth of grass or weeds, other than trees, shrubs, cultivated flowers, gardens, and any vegetation that is part of an approved stormwater management plan, to exceed a height of twelve (12) inches or more, or to permit or allow on the premises the growth of any other deleterious or unhealthful vegetation. It shall be a violation and declared a nuisance if any growth of flowers, gardens, or otherwise, is allowed in an uncontrolled manner or is not regularly maintained.
(Ord. No. 23636, § 1, 9-2-09; Ord. No. 25405 , § 3, 8-9-17)

Sec. 8-4-112.13. - Protective treatment.
SHARE LINK TO SECTION
PRINT SECTION
DOWNLOAD (DOCX) OF SECTIONS
EMAIL SECTION
COMPARE VERSIONS
All exterior surfaces, including but not limited to, doors, door and window frames, cornices, porches, trim, balconies, decks and fences shall be maintained in good condition. Exterior wood surfaces, other than decay-resistant woods, shall be protected from the elements and decay by painting or other protective covering or treatment. Peeling, flaking and chipped paint shall be eliminated and surfaces repainted. All siding and masonry joints as well as those between the building envelope and the perimeter of windows, doors, and skylights shall be maintained weather resistant and water tight. All metal surfaces subject to rust or corrosion and all surfaces with rust or corrosion shall be stabilized and coated to inhibit future rust and corrosion. Exterior property showing thirty-three (33) percent or more of damage or deterioration shall be deemed a nuisance and subject to the procedures and penalties as provided in the Code of Ordinances Chapter 19, Article VI (Dangerous Buildings).
(Ord. No. 23636, § 1, 9-2-09)
Sec. 8-4-112.14. - Premises identification.
SHARE LINK TO SECTION
PRINT SECTION
DOWNLOAD (DOCX) OF SECTIONS
EMAIL SECTION
COMPARE VERSIONS
(a)
All structures, whether vacant or occupied, must post and maintain in a permanent, durable and substantial manner, and in a position to be plainly legible and visible from the street or road for which the address has been assigned, the official municipal address. The municipal number shall contrast with their background to ensure visibility. Municipal numbers shall be Arabic numerals which comply with size provisions set forth in section 8-3-108.
(b)
Single occupancy structures shall bear one (1) official municipal address and multi-use structures shall bear any and all official municipal numbers recognized for the structure or structures located on the legal lot of record.
(c)
Failure to comply with this section shall be considered a prohibited activity and a nuisance. The owner of a property in violation of this section shall be subject to a fine in the amount of two hundred fifty dollars ($250.00) for the first violation, five hundred dollars ($500.00) for the second violation and one thousand dollars ($1,000.00) for each subsequent violation and/or ten (10) days in jail.
(d)
All provisions specified in section 8-3-108, survey and street address remain in effect and should be used in conjunction with the provisions herein.
(Ord. No. 23636, § 1, 9-2-09)
