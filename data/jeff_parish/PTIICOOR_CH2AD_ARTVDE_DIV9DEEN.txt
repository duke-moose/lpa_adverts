(a)
Qualifications. The director of engineering shall possess the following qualifications:
(1)
A four-year degree in engineering and active registration as a professional engineer in the state;
(2)
Ten (10) years' experience in engineering; and
(3)
Three (3) years' experience in local government operations or consulting work.
(b)
Appointment. The director of engineering shall be appointed by the parish president with the approval of the parish council.
(c)
Salary. The salary of the director of engineering shall be established by the parish president within the pay grade for the position as defined in the executive pay plan.
(Ord. No. 17553, § 3, 7-13-88; Ord. No. 24699, § 1, 3-19-14; Ord. No. 25736 , § 16, 2-6-19)
Editor's note— Ord. No. 25736 , § 16, adopted Feb. 6, 2019, amended § 2-242 and in so doing changed the title from "Qualifications and appointment of director" to "Qualifications, appointment and salary of director," as set out herein.
Sec. 2-243. - Duties of the director.

The director of engineering shall assist the public works director in directing, controlling and supervising the following functions:
(1)
Engineering design, construction supervision and project management of all projects under the jurisdiction of the public works department;
(2)
Construction supervision of all water, sewerage, streets, and drainage infrastructure installation and all such facilities accepted for maintenance by the parish from private developments;
(3)
Traffic engineering and the associated design and maintenance of signals, signs and markings;
(4)
Street lighting with associated design, operation, and maintenance;
(5)
Planning of future, major street and bridge capital improvement projects, including maintenance and new construction;
(6)
Provide engineering services to the department of public works as required and directed by the director of the department of public works;
(7)
Surveying;
(8)
SCADA (supervisory control and automated data acquisition) system design and maintenance;
(9)
Public works warehouse facility operations and maintenance.
(Ord. No. 17553, § 4, 7-13-88; Ord. No. 23213, § 3, 12-12-07; Ord. No. 24699, § 2, 3-19-14)
