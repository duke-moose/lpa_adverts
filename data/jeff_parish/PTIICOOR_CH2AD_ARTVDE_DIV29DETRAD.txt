The department of transit administration and the position of director of transit administration are hereby created, which department and position shall have a direct administrative supervision over public transit functions of the parish.

Sec. 2-514. - Qualifications, appointment and salary of director.

(a)
Qualifications. The director of transit administration will be qualified by education, training and prior administrative and/or management experience.
(b)
Appointment. The director of transit administration shall be appointed by the parish president with the approval of the council.
(c)
Salary. The salary of the director of the department of transit shall be established by the parish president within the pay grade for the position as defined in the executive pay plan.
(Ord. No. 24725, § 2—4, 4-9-14; Ord. No. 25736 , § 40, 2-6-19)
Editor's note— Ord. No. 25736 , § 40, adopted Feb. 6, 2019, amended § 2-514 and in so doing changed the title from "Qualifications and appointment of director" to "Qualifications, appointment and salary of director," as set out herein.
Sec. 2-515. - Duties of director.

The director of transit administration shall:
(1)
Manage department operations in transit administration;
(2)
Provide policy guidance to the parish president and chief operating officer in the area of public transit;
(3)
Direct administrative and field staff assigned to this department;
(4)
Formulate and evaluate operating policies, programs and procedures relating to public transit, with advice and consent of the chief operating officer;
(5)
Coordinate budget formulation and management activities in the department of transit administration;
(6)
Prepare, file and administer all federal, state, and regional transit operating and capital grant programs;
(7)
Monitor transit-related contracts;
(8)
Seek out funding sources and develop programs to insure sufficient nonfarebox revenue is available to carry out transit programs;
(9)
Evaluate and report on a regular basis to the parish president and the chief operating officer regarding information on the operations of the department of transit administration;
(10)
Coordinate and prepare reports on departmental operations evaluating performance against established objectives, and special reports on operating problems or plans as required.
(Ord. No. 24725, § 2—4, 4-9-14)
