There is hereby created the department of property maintenance zoning/quality of life to serve the Parish of Jefferson and to oversee all inspection and enforcement responsibilities related to Jefferson Parish zoning and code ordinances regarding the use and maintenance of privately owned property and structures, overseeing the weed control division, and taxicab regulations.
(Ord. No. 24726, § 2, 4-9-14)

(a)
Qualifications. The director of property maintenance zoning/quality of life shall be qualified by a bachelor's degree in architecture; civil engineering; planning; law or related field, supplemented by extensive, successful, full-time paid experience; or an equivalent combination of five (5) years of supervisory experience, education and training in related field.
(b)
Appointment. The director of property maintenance zoning/quality of life shall be appointed by the parish president with the approval of the council.
(c)
Salary. The salary of the director of property maintenance zoning/quality of life shall be established by the parish president within the pay grade for the position as defined in the executive pay plan.
(Ord. No. 24726, § 2, 4-9-14; Ord. No. 25736 , § 11, 2-6-19)
Editor's note— Ord. No. 25736 , § 11, adopted Feb. 6, 2019, amended § 2-201 and in so doing changed the title from "Qualifications, appointment and salary for the director of property maintenance zoning/quality of life director" to "Qualifications, appointment and salary of director," as set out herein.
Sec. 2-202. - Duties of the director of property maintenance zoning/quality of life; generally.

The director of property maintenance zoning/quality of life's duties shall include but are not limited to the following:
(1)
Directs, manages, and oversees the inspection and enforcement of ordinances and codes regarding the use and maintenance of privately owned property and structures.
(2)
Directs, manages and oversees the operations of the weed control division including contracting and budgeting functions.
(3)
Directs, manages and oversees the inspection and enforcement relative to quality of life and taxicab issues.
(4)
Keeps abreast of current developments relative to quality of life and taxicab issues, laws, regulations, codes, processes, plans, and procedures.
(5)
Develops operating budget and ensures department operates within approved budget; monitors budget activity; approves expenditures.
(6)
Meets with and responds to complaints, questions, and concerns from the public, developers, contractors, council members, other elected officials, administrative officers and department heads, sheriffs office and community business leaders within Jefferson Parish and the metropolitan area.
(7)
Creates and implements department policies and procedures to enforce codes, ordinances, and standards. Reviews and recommends code/ordinances changes.
(8)
Manages the adjudication process stemming from ordinance violations forwarded to the parish attorney's office.
(9)
Advises the parish president and parish council relative to quality of life and enforcement issues, weed control issues, taxicab issues, laws, processes, regulations, requirements, and procedures.
(10)
Prepares reports on department operations; evaluates department performance against goals and objectives; prepares special reports as required or directed.
(11)
Attends parish council meetings, civil service hearings, department related meetings, parish directors' meetings, civic group meetings, and any other meetings as required or directed.
(12)
Grants interviews with news media, prepares press releases in collaboration with the parish public information office and prepares other informational materials.
(13)
Performs all other duties as required or directed.
(Ord. No. 24726, § 2, 4-9-14)
