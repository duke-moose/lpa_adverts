The coastal zone management committee shall be composed of twenty-one (21) members and representing as many of the following interests as possible, such as: The oil-gas industry, agriculture and forestry, commercial fishing and trapping, sport fishing, hunting and recreation, ports and shipping, nature preservation—environmental protection, landowners, municipalities, solid minerals, industrial development, parish council member(s), parish planning department, wildlife and fisheries, tourism commission, fish or shellfish processors, and the parish coastal zone management agency. The membership shall be named by and serve at the pleasure of the council.
(Code 1961, § 2-45.12(a), (b))

The coastal zone management committee is established for the purpose of verifying additional information that must be added to the coastal zone management maps, acting as a communication center for public opinion, for technical information coming from several sources, for coordination with the parish council in identifying parish problems, for pointing out changes that need to be made in the state's program, for providing input into the parish's plan based on their level of expertise on the coastal zone, their knowledge of local people, needs and problems and for other inputs into the parish's coastal zone management program and into the state's coastal resources program.
(Code 1961, § 2-45.12(a))
