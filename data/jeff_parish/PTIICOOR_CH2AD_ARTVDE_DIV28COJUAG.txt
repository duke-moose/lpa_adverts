Sec. 2-509. - Agency and position of director created.

A department to be known as the community justice agency and the position of director of the community justice agency are hereby created, which department and position shall have direct administrative supervision over the adult supervision and corrections, the administrative and planning services, and the juvenile justice functions of parish government. The community justice agency shall serve as an umbrella agency to consolidate and operate all community-based and residential juvenile and adult criminal justice programs in parish government (except for the adult jail) and to provide administrative services for the effective operations of such programs.
(Ord. No. 21662, § 1, 9-11-02)

(a)
Qualifications. The director of the community justice agency shall be qualified by education and training, possess a thorough knowledge of the adult and juvenile criminal justice systems, demonstrate an ability to manage administrative operations, possess skills in operations and analysis techniques, have proven oral and written communication skills, demonstrate leadership ability in a local government setting, and have advanced administrative and/or managerial experience in criminal justice research, planning, coordination and evaluation. The director of the community justice agency shall also have the requisite educational and work experiences.
(b)
Appointment. The director of the community justice agency shall be appointed by the parish president with the approval of the parish council.
(c)
Salary. The salary of the director of the community justice agency shall be established by the parish president within the pay grade for the position as defined in the executive pay plan.
(Ord. No. 21662, § 1, 9-11-02; Ord. No. 25736 , § 38, 2-6-19)

Sec. 2-509.2. - Duties of the director of the community justice agency.

The duties of the director shall include but not be limited to the following:
(1)
Develop and operate community-based programs and residential facilities for the adult and juvenile justice systems (except the adult jail); and provide for both the adult and juvenile system's coordination of the continuum of sanctions and services;
(2)
Direct all new or newly-refined adult community-based programs for both pre-trial and convicted offender populations, including but not limited to: pre-trial diversion, release, and supervision programs, alternatives to incarceration, and day reporting and intermediate sanctions facilities;
(3)
Direct the operations of the juvenile justice system, including both community and residential services for juveniles;
(4)
Direct the provision of administrative services to component parts of the agency;
(5)
Ensure coordination of funding requests and program development from component parts of the agency;
(6)
Work with the advisory board as directed by the parish president to provide the parish council with a master plan, analyze operational and programmatic impacts, prioritize recommendations, and review operational budgets of related agencies;
(7)
Work with the appropriate city, parish, state, federal and private agencies concerned with the administration of criminal justice to address criminal justice issues affecting the parish;
(8)
Ensure that the research, planning, and evaluation component of the administrative department conducts and disseminates the results of high-quality research; researches and distributes information about national and international best practices in criminal justice and juvenile justice; evaluates and monitors the effectiveness of all programs operated by the agency; makes recommendations for changes to programs based on the results of such evaluations; collects, analyzes, and publishes statistics and information relevant to criminal justice agencies; and serves as a resource and source of technical assistance to public officials and local units of government in determining policies, procedures, laws and regulations and in evaluating programs operated by other related agencies; and
(9)
Direct the operations of an automated accident reporting system/court case management system and related functions.
(Ord. No. 21662, § 1, 9-11-02)
