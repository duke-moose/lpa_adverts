Sec. 7-209. - Lake Pontchartrain Causeway Bird Sanctuary—Created; boundaries.

PRINT SECTION

The Lake Pontchartrain Causeway Bird Sanctuary is hereby established within the area bounded by the following lines:
On the north, by the line intersecting at right angles the Greater New Orleans Expressway bridge over Lake Pontchartrain one (1) mile from the point at which said bridge crosses the south shore of Lake Pontchartrain; on the south, by the southern shore of Lake Pontchartrain; on the west, by a line west of and parallel to the Greater New Orleans Expressway bridge; and on the east, by a line east of and parallel to the Greater New Orleans Expressway bridge.
(Ord. No. 18090, § 1, 8-29-90)

In addition to the prohibitions found in section 7-207, it shall be unlawful for any person to approach with any commercial or recreational boat or vessel within one thousand (1,000) feet of the purple martin roosting areas along the Greater New Orleans Expressway bridge over Lake Pontchartrain within the Lake Pontchartrain Causeway Bird Sanctuary during the period beginning fifteen (15) minutes before sunset and ending fifteen (15) minutes after sunrise, except for scientific purposes under authority of a permit duly issued by the Louisiana Department of Wildlife and Fisheries.
(Ord. No. 18090, § 2, 8-29-90)
