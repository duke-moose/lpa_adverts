Sec. 36-204. - Definitions.
SHARE LINK TO SECTION
PRINT SECTION
DOWNLOAD (DOCX) OF SECTIONS
EMAIL SECTION
COMPARE VERSIONS
The following words and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings respectively ascribed to them:
Boards shall mean any material used to alter the height of the sidewalls to a height equal to that of the tailgate.
Box shall mean that part of the truck body that contains and confines the bulk material.
Inspector shall mean any person representing the parish on the project for the purpose of tabulating and approving the volume of material used in the project.
Material shall mean any bulk construction material, such as river-sand, clam shell, sand-shell mixtures, etc., for which payment is to be made on a delivered volume basis.
Project shall mean any construction activity in the parish for which the parish council is the legal administrative authority or for which the parish is the legal owner.
Sidewall shall mean the sides of the box.
Tailgate shall mean the rear wall of the box.
Truck shall mean any vehicle used in the transportation of bulk construction materials.
(Code 1961, § 11-112)

The application form shall contain blank spaces for use by the certifying personnel in recording the interior length, width, and height of the box; the height shall be recorded both with and without boards. Sec. 36-222. - Display.

(a)
Permit sticker. A permit sticker shall be issued containing the permit number, the expiration date, and the pay-volumes. This sticker is to be affixed to the windshield of the truck cab by the certifying officials.
(b)
Permit plate. A permit plate shall be issued containing the permit number, the box dimensions, and the pay-volumes. This plate is to be affixed to the box by the certifying officials.
(c)
Display. Prior to depositing the material on the project site, the truck driver must display the windshield sticker and/or the box plate to the inspector to the satisfaction of the inspector. If either the sticker or the plate is missing, mutilated, or illegible, the driver must display the original permit to the inspector.
(Code 1961, § 11-118)
