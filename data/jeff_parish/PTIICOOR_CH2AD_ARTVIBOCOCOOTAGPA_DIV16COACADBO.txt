The community action advisory board shall be composed of twenty-one (21) members and seven (7) alternates, as follows:
(1)
The parish president or his designee;
(2)
A councilperson at large Division A or B and councilpersons from Districts 1, 2, and 3 or an appointed representatives of the council at large Division A or B and representative of Districts 1, 2 and 3 each, as designated by the council. The council must fill their appointed seats if there is a vacancy within 100 days of vacancy;
(3)
A representative from the division of children and family services;
(4)
Sheriff of the parish, or his designated representative;
(5)
A representative on behalf of a member of the local judiciary bodies;
(6)
One (1) representative and one (1) alternate designated by the Marrero Community/Senior Center;
(7)
One (1) representative and one (1) alternate designated by the Hazel Rhea Hurst Community Center;
(8)
One (1) representative and one (1) alternate designated by the Dorothy Watson Center;
(9)
One (1) representative and one (1) alternate designated by the Bridge City Community Center;
(10)
One (1) representative and one (1) alternate designated by the J.C. Simmons, Sr. Community Center;
(11)
One (1) representative and one (1) alternate designated by the Harvey Community Center;
(12)
One (1) representative and one (1) alternate designated by the Gretna Community Center;
(13)
One (1) representative of the Parish Community Programs Departments;
(14)
One (1) representative from the Parish electric utility provider;
(15)
One (1) representative of the Head Start Policy Council;
(16)
Three (3) representatives shall serve as at-large members at the request of the Director of JeffCAP.
(Code 1961, § 2-100; Ord. No. 17961, § 1, 4-4-90; Ord. No. 18462, § 1, 2-5-92; Ord. No. 24832 , § 3, 9-17-14)

The staff director of the C.A.A., who shall be responsible for the day-to-day operation of the program, including all matters pertaining to personnel and finances, shall be appointed by the parish president and the appointee shall be subject to the approval of the parish council.

Sec. 2-735. - Duties of; compliance with O.E.O. regulations; budget approval; exception from civil service; personal regulations.
SHARE LINK TO SECTION

(a)
The community action advisory board shall be advisory in nature and a body that disseminates information to the community. The board must remain a tripartite (three (3) sectors of the community) while active. All fiduciary, contractual, grant application or agreement decisions for the C.A.A. will come by approval of the Parish Council. All grant oversight and C.A.A. program content will be decided upon by the Director of the C.A.A. whom is the parish president's at-will appointee, under advisement from parish administration and the C.A.A. advisory board. The C.A.A. board will bear no fiduciary or programmatic responsibility. The board will approve reports as delivered to them by JEFFCAP staff.
(b)
In this regard and considering employment under this program, and as the board consists of appointed members, it shall be exempt from any and all civil service regulations and personnel regulations of the parish, and shall comply solely with the regulations and guidelines of Economic Opportunity Act, as amended.
(Code 1961, § 2-106; Ord. No. 24832 , § 5, 9-17-14)

In all instances where the authority or approval of any elected officials in the parish shall be required, such officials shall give due and proper notice to the administrative board of its intentions to act and shall provide at least ten (10) days' notice to the administrative board, and further provide for public hearings in all such matters.

(a)
The community action board shall have full authority and power to contract within the limitations provided in O.E.O. regulations and in all matters of contract, such contracts shall be within the approval of the parish council.
(b)
The community action board shall have the authority to appoint neighborhood based organizations and other groups within the area and all necessary communities and boards which are necessary to carry out the purposes of the board.
