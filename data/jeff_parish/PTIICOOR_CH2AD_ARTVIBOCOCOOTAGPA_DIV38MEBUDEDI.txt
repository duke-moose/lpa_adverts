Jefferson Parish has created a special taxing district and political subdivision of the state, known as the "Metairie Business Development District" referred to in this section as the "district." The district shall be comprised of the property in Jefferson Parish bounded by the north side of Veterans Memorial Boulevard, the west side of Causeway Boulevard, the south side of west bound West Esplanade Avenue, and Division Street and the development sites fronting on the west side of Division Street. For the purposes of this district, development site shall mean any group of one (1) or more lots occupied or intended for development as a unit and fronting shall mean a development site with a lot line common to the street right-of-way line."
(Ord. No. 24919 , § 4, 3-4-15)

The district shall be established for the primary purpose of promoting, encouraging, and participating in infrastructure improvements to projects or plans to stimulate the economy through commerce, industry, and research and for the utilization and development of natural, physical, and human resources of the area. The district shall not promote, encourage, or participate in any action to assist in the development or redevelopment of a hotel including infrastructure located on a site to be developed for a hotel; however, nothing shall prohibit the district from providing public infrastructure that benefits the public generally outside the development site of any hotel.
(Ord. No. 24919 , § 4, 3-4-15)
Sec. 2-875.49 - Board.

(a)
Creation. The board shall be composed of nine (9) members, all of whom shall be qualified voters of the parish and at least seven (7) of whom shall have their principal place of business in, their principal domicile in, or own property in, the district. Such members shall possess additional qualifications and shall be appointed as follows:
(1)
One (1) of the members shall be appointed by the parish president.
(2)
One (1) of the members shall be appointed by the Jefferson Parish Sheriff.
(3)
Two (2) of the members shall be appointed by the at-large members of the parish council, whereby each at-large councilperson shall appoint one member.
(4)
Three (3) of the members shall be appointed by the District five councilperson.
(5)
One (1) member shall be appointed by the state senator representing the area or a majority of the area of the district.
(6)
One (1) member shall be appointed by the state representative representing the area or a majority of the area of the district.
(7)
The members of the board initially appointed shall be appointed as follows: three (3) members for two (2) years each, three (3) members for three (3) years each, and three (3) members for four (4) years each, the length of the term for each individual appointed to be determined by lot. They shall serve until their successors have been appointed and qualified.
(8)
The members of the board thereafter appointed, upon the expiration of the respective terms of the initial appointees, shall be selected and appointed in accordance with the procedures herein prescribed for the selection and appointment of the original members for the term of five (5) years. Any vacancy which occurs prior to the expiration of the term for which a member of the board has been appointed shall be filled in accordance with the procedures as set forth herein.
(9)
All members, except two (2) of the members appointed by the District 5 councilperson, shall have their principal place of business in, their principal domicile in, or own property in the district.
(Ord. No. 24919 , § 4, 3-4-15)
Sec. 2-875.50 - Membership and meetings.

(a)
The board of the district shall exercise the powers granted to the district pursuant to R.S. 33:1420.19. Each member shall hold office for a term of five (5) years and until a successor is chosen and qualifies.
(b)
As soon as practicable after its appointment, the board shall meet and elect from their number a chairman, a vice chairman, a treasurer, and such other officers as it may deem appropriate. A secretary of the board may be selected from among the members or may be otherwise selected or employed by the board. The duties of the said officers shall be fixed by bylaws adopted by the board.
(c)
The board shall adopt such rules and regulations as it deems necessary or advisable for conducting its business and affairs, and may engage such assistants and employees as is needed to assist the board in the performance of its duties.
(d)
It shall hold regular meetings as shall be provided by its bylaws and may hold special meetings at such time and places within or without the district as may be prescribed in its rules or regulations.
(e)
A majority of the members of the board shall constitute a quorum for the transaction of business.
(f)
The board shall keep a permanent record book in which shall be recorded minutes of all meetings, proceedings, and any and all corporate acts. The record book shall be opened to inspection in accordance with R.S. 44:31 et seq.
(g)
Each board member may be entitled to receive for his or her services a per diem per meeting and such travel expenses as may be authorized in the bylaws of the district.
(h)
All meetings of the board shall be conducted in accordance with R.S. 42:11 et seq.
(i)
The board shall adopt rules of procedure not in conflict with any state act or parish ordinance.
(Ord. No. 24919 , § 4, 3-4-15)

(a)
The board may employ and fix the compensation of a district manager. If employed, the district manager shall have charge and supervision of the works of the district and shall be responsible for preserving and maintaining any improvement or facility constructed or erected pursuant to the provisions of this section, for maintaining and operating the equipment owned by the district, and for performing such other duties as may be prescribed by the board. The district manager may hire or otherwise employ and terminate the employment of such other persons, including, without limitation, professional, supervisory, and clerical employees, as may be necessary and authorized by the board. The compensation and other conditions of employment of the officers and employees of the district shall be as provided by the board. Notwithstanding R.S. 42:1101 et seq., the district manager or a board member or district employee may be a stockholder, officer, or employee of a landowner.
(b)
The treasurer of the district shall have charge of the funds of the district. Such funds shall be disbursed only upon the order of the board by warrant or check countersigned by the treasurer and by such other person as may be authorized by the board. The board may give the treasurer such other or additional powers and duties as the board may deem appropriate. The financial records of the district shall be audited by an independent certified public accountant at least once a year.
(c)
The board may select as a depository for its funds any qualified public depository as defined in R.S. 39:1211 et seq.
(Ord. No. 24919 , § 4, 3-4-15)

Sec. 2-875.53 - Development activities.

(a)
In order to effectuate the purposes of the district, the board shall have the specific authority provided in RS 33:1420.19 (H); such authority shall be exercised solely within the district.
(b)
The board shall collaborate with parish government to prepare, implement, and maintain a redevelopment plan for the district and a program to implement the redevelopment plan, which shall be a part of the parish's comprehensive plan. The program shall address capital improvements and shall implement the plan in such a manner as to aid and encourage private development of the area and to promote and coordinate public development. In collaborating with parish government, the board may conduct studies and may consult with all departments of Jefferson Parish and other public or private agencies concerned with matters affecting or affected by the program.
(c)
After adoption of the development program, the board may implement any portion thereof in such manner as shall, in its judgment, most likely accomplish said program. To that end, the district may employ or contract with contractors, engineers, architects, attorneys, underwriters, and other professionals in accordance with state law.
(d)
The board shall prepare each year an annual budget in accordance with the Louisiana Local Government Budget Act, R.S. 39:1301 et seq.
(Ord. No. 24919 , § 4, 3-4-15)
